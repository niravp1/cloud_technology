Mauna Loa

Mauna Loa, located on the Big Island of Hawaii, is one of the most massive and impressive shield volcanoes on Earth. It is the largest active volcano on Earth, standing at an impressive 13,678 feet (4,169 meters) above sea level. When measured from its base on the ocean floor, Mauna Loa reaches a staggering height of over 30,000 feet (9,144 meters), making it the tallest mountain on the planet. Mauna Loa is classified as a shield volcano due to its broad, gently sloping profile that resembles a warrior's shield. This shape is formed by successive layers of low-viscosity basaltic lava flows.

St Helens

Mount St. Helens is part of the Cascade Range of volcanoes in the Pacific Northwest. Standing at an elevation of 8,363 feet (2,549 meters), it was once a beautifully symmetrical mountain, known for its picturesque conical shape. However, on May 18, 1980, everything changed. In a catastrophic eruption, the mountain's north face collapsed, resulting in a lateral blast that devastated an area of over 230 square miles (600 square kilometers). The eruption and ensuing pyroclastic flows, ashfall, and mudflows claimed 57 lives and dramatically altered the landscape.
