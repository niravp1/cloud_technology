List of Other Activites

Skinning, a cherished tradition in Finland, embodies the heart of Nordic culture and connection to nature. As the winter months cast their icy spell across the pristine landscapes, skining enthusiasts eagerly don their gear, seeking the thrill of gliding through the snow-covered terrain. Finland's vast wilderness, dotted with ancient forests and glistening lakes, offers a picturesque backdrop for this time-honored activity. Skining trails wind through silent woods, where the only sound is the soft swish of skis against the powdery snow. In the heart of Lapland, the northernmost region of Finland, skining takes on a magical quality under the dancing hues of the Northern Lights, creating an ethereal atmosphere that captivates both locals and visitors alike. With its seamless blend of tranquility and adventure, skining in Finland is an experience that not only invigorates the body but also nourishes the soul.

Must Do Adventures

Saunas are an integral part of Finnish culture, providing a sanctuary for relaxation, contemplation, and socialization. In Finland, saunas are not simply a luxury, but a way of life. With over two million saunas in the country, they outnumber cars and are deeply ingrained in the Finnish way of living. The traditional wood-burning saunas emit a soothing heat that purifies both body and mind. After a leisurely session, Finns often take a refreshing plunge into a nearby lake or roll in the snow, a ritual that invigorates and rejuvenates. The experience is as much about physical well-being as it is about communing with nature and embracing a profound sense of tranquility.

Must Do Adventures

Suomenlinna, located just off the coast of Helsinki, is a UNESCO World Heritage site that encapsulates centuries of Finnish history and maritime prowess. This fortified sea fortress, often referred to as the "Gibraltar of the North," stands as a testament to Finland's strategic importance in the Baltic region. Originally built in the mid-18th century, Suomenlinna boasts impressive walls, tunnels, and cannon ramparts, all set against the stunning backdrop of the Gulf of Finland. Today, the island serves as a living museum, housing a vibrant community of residents, museums, restaurants, and cultural events. Visitors can explore the island's rich past while enjoying breathtaking views of the surrounding archipelago, making Suomenlinna a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a taste of Finland's historical and natural splendor.
